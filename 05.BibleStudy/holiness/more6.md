The full context runs **1 Peter 2:9–10:**

> *"But ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people; that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light: Which in time past were not a people, but are now the people of God: which had not obtained mercy, but now have obtained mercy."*

---

## Why This Matters — And How It Connects to What We've Been Studying

This verse is **directly relevant to the three entry points of sanctification** we just mapped.

Look at what Peter is doing:

### Four Identity Declarations

| Declaration | KJV | What It Means |
|-------------|-----|---------------|
| *Chosen generation* | *genos eklekton* | Elected — your identity is determined by God's choice, not your achievement |
| *Royal priesthood* | *basileion hierateuma* | You have direct access to God AND you represent God to the world — both directions |
| *Holy nation* | *ethnos hagion* | *Hagion* — the sanctification word. You are collectively set apart — a people whose defining characteristic is holiness |
| *Peculiar people* | *laos eis peripoiēsin* | A people for God's own possession — His treasured property |

### The Purpose Clause — Why All Four

Peter gives the reason in the same verse:

> *"that ye should shew forth the praises of him who hath called you out of darkness into his marvellous light."*

The four identity declarations are not the goal — they are the **means** to the goal. The goal is **declaring the excellencies of God** to a watching world. This connects directly to Matthew 5:14–16 — *"Ye are the light of the world… let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven."*

---

## The Connection to Our Three Entry Points

| Entry Point | Our Study | 1 Peter 2:9 |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Poor in spirit** (Matt 5:3) | You bring nothing — empty-handed before God | *"called you out of darkness"* — you were in darkness; God called, you didn't climb |
| **Heart diagnosed** (Mark 7:21) | The heart is the source of defilement | *"holy nation"* — *hagion* — the heart that is set apart; sanctification is the people's defining mark |
| **Body governed** (1 Thess 4:3) | Abstain — visible, bodily holiness | *"shew forth the praises"* — the holy life is a public declaration; the body's behavior is the testimony |

And notice verse 10:

> *"Which in time past were not a people, but are now the people of God: which had not obtained mercy, but now have obtained mercy."*

This is the **before/after** that makes all three entry points possible:
- **Before:** not a people, no mercy, in darkness — which is the condition of the heart left to itself (Mark 7:21)
- **After:** chosen, royal, holy, peculiar, in light — which is the condition of the Spirit-indwelt, sanctified believer (1 Thess 4:7–8)

The transition between before and after is exactly **poverty of spirit** (Matt 5:3) — the recognition that you were *"not a people"* and had *"not obtained mercy."* You brought nothing. God gave everything.

---

## The OT Root — Peter Is Quoting Exodus 19:5–6

Peter is directly quoting God's words to Israel at Sinai:

> *"Now therefore, if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me above all people: for all the earth is mine: And ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation."* — Exodus 19:5–6 (KJV)

What God declared over Israel at Sinai, Peter declares over the **church** — the new covenant people of God. The identity has not changed; the *scope* has expanded. What was spoken over one nation is now spoken over every tribe, tongue, and nation that is in Christ.

This also ties directly back to the **missiological note** from our Matthew 5 study: the church's holiness is its witness. Israel was meant to be a *kingdom of priests* — mediating God to the nations. The church inherits that calling. The holy life is the missionary life.

---

## One Connecting Statement

**First Peter 2:9 is the identity declaration that makes the three entry points of sanctification make sense: you are already chosen, royal, holy, and possessed by God — therefore, come poor in spirit (you brought nothing to earn this), own your heart (the darkness you were called out of was internal), and govern your body (shew forth the praises — your life is the testimony).**
