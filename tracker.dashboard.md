Finding a financial tracker that perfectly balances **daily budgeting, cash flow forecasting, and long-term tools like a mortgage calculator** narrows the field significantly. While almost all top-tier apps track income and expenses, very few feature built-in *sandbox calculators* for running "what-if" mortgage scenarios directly alongside your live data. 

Here are the best financial trackers on the market right now that meet your exact criteria, categorized by their strengths:

### 1. The All-in-One Powerhouse: Quicken Classic (Deluxe or Premier)
If you want absolutely everything on your list native to one piece of software, Quicken Classic is the undisputed king. Originally a desktop software (now with a web and mobile companion app), it is built for people who want total control over complex finances.
*   **Budget, Income & Expense:** Highly customizable budgeting and expense tracking.
*   **Cash Flow:** Excellent cash flow projections and calendar views so you can see your exact balance 30, 60, or 90 days from now. 
*   **Mortgage Calculator:** It is one of the only platforms with dedicated, built-in loan and mortgage calculators. You can run amortization schedules, see how extra principal payments shorten your loan, or calculate scenarios for buying a new house.
*   **Etc:** Deep investment tracking, tax reporting, and bill management.

### 2. The Best Modern App: Monarch Money
Monarch Money is widely considered the best modern personal finance app (and the primary landing spot for former Mint users). It is highly visual, heavily automated, and constantly updated.
*   **Budget, Income & Expense:** Incredible interface with custom categories, rules, and roll-up budgeting. You can share it seamlessly with a partner.
*   **Cash Flow:** Features beautiful "Sankey" diagrams (showing exactly where your money flowed) and month-to-month cash flow tracking.
*   **Mortgage Tracking:** While it doesn’t have a generic "calculator," it integrates directly with your mortgage lender and Zillow. It automatically calculates your home equity, tracks your interest rate, and shows your mortgage payoff progress.
*   **Etc:** Custom dashboard, goal tracking, and investment syncing.

### 3. The Best for Future Projections: PocketSmith
If "cash flow" is your primary concern, PocketSmith is built entirely around forecasting. 
*   **Cash Flow & Budget:** It uses a calendar-based approach to project your daily bank balances up to *30 years* into the future based on your scheduled income and expenses.
*   **Mortgage Scenario Testing:** Because of its forecasting engine, you can run "what-if" scenarios. For example, you can test: *"What happens to my cash flow in 5 years if I take on a $3,000/month mortgage today?"*
*   **Income & Expense:** Connects to banks globally to track current spending.

### 4. The Ultimate Customization: Tiller (Spreadsheets)
If you want a mortgage calculator that completely interacts with your daily budget, a spreadsheet is the only way to get pixel-perfect customization. 
*   **How it works:** Tiller securely connects to your banks, credit cards, and mortgage lenders, and automatically downloads your daily transactions into Google Sheets or Microsoft Excel.
*   **Budget & Cash Flow:** Tiller provides a pre-built "Foundation Template" that handles all your automated income, expense, and cash flow tracking.
*   **Mortgage Calculator:** Because you are in a spreadsheet, you can plug in any advanced mortgage calculator, debt-snowball, or debt-avalanche template and feed it directly with your live financial data.

### 5. Best for Net Worth & Retirement: Empower (Formerly Personal Capital)
*   **Budget, Income & Expense:** It tracks income and expenses automatically, though its actual "budgeting" tools are fairly basic compared to Monarch.
*   **Cash Flow & Etc:** Incredible cash flow charts, fee analyzers, and investment checkups. 
*   **Mortgage & Retirement Calculators:** It has the best free retirement planner/calculator on the market. You can add scenarios like "Buy a home for $500,000 in 2028" and the algorithm will calculate how that mortgage affects your lifetime cash flow and retirement. 
*   **Price:** Completely free to use (they make money by offering you optional wealth management services).

### Summary Recommendation:
*   Choose **Quicken Classic** if you want deep, built-in loan calculators and don't mind a traditional desktop-first interface.
*   Choose **Monarch Money** if you want a beautiful, modern app on your phone to track your cash flow, budget, and live mortgage equity.
*   Choose **Tiller** if you are a spreadsheet nerd who wants total control over your own financial calculators.
