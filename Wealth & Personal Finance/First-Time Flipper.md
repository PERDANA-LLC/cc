## PART 1 — Mindset & Foundation (Days 1–7)

### The Myth That's Costing You Wealth

Most people sit on the sidelines for years waiting until they have "enough" money. Meanwhile, first-time investors with $8k–$15k are closing deals in secondary markets, house-hacking their way to free rent, and building portfolios quietly.

The game isn't about having money. It's about **controlling assets with other people's money**.

### The BRRR-Lite Strategy (Your Blueprint)

BRRR = **Buy. Renovate. Rent. Repeat.**

For beginners with under $15k, you run a simplified version:
1. **Buy** a distressed property using low-money-down financing
2. **Renovate** cosmetically only (paint, floors, fixtures — no structural)
3. **Rent** it out immediately at market rate
4. **Refinance** later to pull your cash back out (optional at this stage)

You're not flipping to sell. You're flipping the condition to hold.

## PART 2 — Deal Finding Hacks (Days 8–30)

### 5 Ways to Find Below-Market Deals Without a License

**1. Drive for Dollars**
Physically drive neighborhoods and note boarded-up homes, overgrown yards, or properties with code violation notices. Look up the owner on your county tax assessor website. Mail a handwritten letter: *"I buy houses as-is. Cash offer within 7 days."*

**2. Probate Leads**
When someone dies, their estate often includes property heirs don't want to manage. Go to your county courthouse (or online probate records) and search recent filings. Contact the executor directly.

**3. Wholesale Deals**
Wholesalers find deals and assign contracts for a fee ($3k–$10k). Join your local REIA (Real Estate Investors Association) and connect with wholesalers. They hand you deals — you just need the ability to close.

**4. Facebook Marketplace + Craigslist**
Search: "house for sale by owner," "fixer upper," "motivated seller." Respond fast. Sellers here often haven't called an agent yet.

**5. Tax-Delinquent List**
Contact your county tax office and request the delinquent tax list. These owners are behind on taxes — often desperate to sell before the county forecloses.

### Is It Actually a Deal? The Two-Number Test

**The 1% Rule:** Monthly rent should equal at least 1% of purchase price.
- Buy at $80k → needs to rent for $800/month minimum ✓

**MAO Formula (Maximum Allowable Offer):**
`MAO = (After Repair Value × 0.70) − Repair Costs`
- ARV: $110k → × 0.70 = $77k → minus $15k repairs = **$62k MAO**

Never pay above your MAO. Walk away if the seller won't budge.

> **Quick Win:** Run the 1% rule on the 5 Zillow properties you found. Which ones pass?

## PART 3 — Financing with Under $15k (Days 8–30, Parallel Track)

### Option 1: FHA House-Hack (Best for W-2 Earners)
- **Down payment:** 3.5% on a 2–4 unit property
- **How it works:** You live in one unit. Tenants pay your mortgage.
- **$15k covers:** 3.5% down on a $200k duplex = $7k + closing costs ~$5k = **$12k total**
- **Hack:** Ask seller to cover closing costs as part of negotiation (seller concessions up to 6%)

### Option 2: Conventional with 5% Down
- Requires 620+ credit score
- Works on single-family homes
- On a $100k property: $5k down + ~$3k closing = **$8k total**

### Option 3: Private Money / Hard Money (For Non-Owner-Occupied)
- Hard money lenders fund 65–80% of ARV
- You bring 20–35% — typically from a private lender (family, friend, business partner)
- Short-term loan (6–12 months), then refinance into conventional

### Option 4: Seller Financing
- Seller acts as the bank
- Negotiate: 0–5% down, monthly payments to seller
- Best for off-market deals with motivated sellers who own free-and-clear

> **Quick Win:** Pull your free credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com. Know your score before any lender does.

## PART 4 — The Renovation Playbook (Days 31–75)

### The Golden Rule: **Cosmetic Only**

Never touch:
- Foundation
- Roof (unless catastrophic and priced in)
- Electrical panels (unless required for occupancy permit)
- Plumbing behind walls

Always address:
- Interior paint (entire unit — one neutral color, e.g., Agreeable Gray)
- Flooring (LVP over everything — $1.50–$2.50/sq ft)
- Light fixtures (swap builder-grade for brushed nickel — $25/fixture)
- Cabinet hardware (swap for black or brushed nickel — $3/pull)
- Bathroom vanity (replace the whole vanity, not just the faucet — $150 at Home Depot)

### The 7 High-ROI Upgrades Under $500 Each

| Upgrade | Cost | Perceived Value Add |
|---|---|---|
| Full interior repaint | $200–$400 (DIY) | +$5,000 perceived value |
| LVP flooring (500 sq ft) | $750–$1,250 | +$8,000 perceived value |
| Kitchen faucet swap | $75–$150 | Fresh/modern look |
| New light fixtures (5) | $125–$250 | +Modernity, brighter |
| Cabinet hardware | $50–$100 | Instant kitchen refresh |
| Bathroom vanity | $150–$300 | Biggest visual impact |
| Curb appeal (mulch + trim) | $100–$200 | First impressions |

**Total budget: $1,450–$2,650 for a full cosmetic renovation** on a 900 sq ft unit.

### Managing Contractors Without Getting Burned

- Get **3 bids minimum** — the middle bid is usually the fair one
- **Never pay more than 10% upfront** — pay in milestones tied to completion
- Use a **scope of work document** (written, signed) before any work begins
- Visit the site **every 2 days** — your presence alone keeps contractors on schedule
- **$500 holdback clause** — last payment only after you do final walkthrough and sign off

> **Quick Win:** Write a 1-page scope of work for the property you're targeting. List every item that needs to be done, room by room.

## PART 5 — Rent It Out Fast (Days 60–90)

### Price to Minimize Vacancy (Not Maximize Rent)

A vacancy costs you more than underpricing by $50/month.

- Research Rentometer.com and Zillow Rent Zestimate for your zip
- Set rent at the **50th percentile** (not the top) to get 10–20 applications in the first week
- A unit sitting empty for 30 days at $950/month costs you $950. Pricing at $895 instead of $950 costs you $660/year — still cheaper than one vacancy month.

### Tenant Screening — Non-Negotiables

Always require:
- [ ] Credit check (minimum 580 for a starter property)
- [ ] Income verification: gross income ≥ 3× monthly rent
- [ ] Rental history: call previous landlords (don't just read the reference)
- [ ] Background check (use SmartMove or RentSpree — tenant pays the fee)

**The One Question That Screens 80% of Problems:**
*"Why are you leaving your current place?"*
Listen carefully. Vague answers or blame = red flag.

### 3 Lease Clauses That Protect You

1. **Late fee clause** — $50 after 5-day grace period, $10/day after that
2. **Lease renewal notice** — tenant must give 60 days written notice before moving out
3. **Property maintenance clause** — tenant responsible for lawn care, filter changes, minor repairs under $75

Not life-changing on one unit. But multiply by 5 properties = $715/month passive. By 10 = $1,430/month. The system scales.

## PART 6 — Tips, Tricks & Hacks Rapid-Fire

### 15 Negotiation Hacks

1. **Always let the seller name the price first** — never anchor low early
2. **Use silence** — after making an offer, stop talking. First to speak loses.
3. **The Nibble:** After agreement, ask for one small thing extra (appliances, closing costs, repairs)
4. **Inspect before negotiating** — use inspection findings to re-negotiate price
5. **Cash offer language** even with financing: "I can close as fast as my lender allows" sounds like cash
6. **30-day close vs. 60-day close** — offer faster close in exchange for lower price
7. **Send offers in writing fast** — speed signals seriousness
8. **The "I need to check with my partner" close** — buys you time without rejection
9. **Motivated seller indicators** — ask "How long has it been on the market?" and "Why are you selling?"
10. **Never show excitement** — stay emotionally neutral in negotiations
11. **Bundle multiple objections** — address them all at once, not one at a time
12. **Escalation clause** — in competitive markets, auto-beat other offers by $500 up to your max
13. **Offer to pay seller's closing costs** in exchange for price reduction
14. **Walk away once** — come back 48 hours later. Seller anxiety does your negotiating.
15. **Every "no" is a negotiating position** — ask "What would it take to make this work?"

### 10 Ways to Cut Renovation Costs by 30%

1. **Shop Habitat for Humanity ReStores** — deep discounts on cabinets, fixtures, appliances
2. **Buy materials yourself, hire labor only** — never let contractors mark up materials
3. **Facebook Marketplace for appliances** — used stainless fridge for $200 vs. $1,200 new
4. **Batch the work** — having a contractor do 3 projects at once = 20–30% discount
5. **YouTube University** — paint, LVP installation, fixture swaps are all learnable in an afternoon
6. **End-of-season sales at Home Depot** — 40–70% off seasonal items
7. **Negotiate contractor payment in cash** — often 10–15% informal discount
8. **Use flat ceilings, not textured** — skip the popcorn ceiling removal entirely (use it as a feature)
9. **Refinish tubs instead of replacing** — $300 refinish vs. $800 replacement
10. **Stage with IKEA/Wayfair basics** — staging photos get 30% more rental inquiries

### How to Force Appreciation in 90 Days or Less

- **Add a bedroom** by closing off a den with a door + closet (requires only a window and closet per most local codes) — adds $15–$25k in value
- **Convert a garage** to a studio ADU (check local zoning first) — adds rental income + value
- **Add washer/dryer hookups** — increases rent $75–$150/month, costs $400–$800
- **Improve curb appeal** before appraisal — mulch, shutters, mailbox = +$2–5k appraised value
- **Get a new appraisal** after cosmetic renovation — document everything with before/after photos

### Decision Tree: When to Walk Away

```
Is the deal at or below MAO?
  NO → Walk away
  YES → Continue

Does it pass the 1% rule?
  NO → Walk away (unless strong appreciation market)
  YES → Continue

Does inspection reveal structural/foundation issues?
  YES → Walk away unless priced at 50¢ on the dollar
  NO → Continue

Can you close within your $15k budget?
  NO → Renegotiate or walk away
  YES → CLOSE THE DEAL
```

### The "Next Property" Formula

After Month 6:
1. **Refinance** if you bought below ARV — pull out your original $15k
2. **Repeat** the same process with the recycled capital
3. **Or HELOC** on the equity for next deal's down payment

Every deal funds the next one. This is how portfolios are built.
