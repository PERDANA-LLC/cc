### 7 Power Hacks Most People Don't Know

**1. The Rapid Rescore Hack**
Once you pay down balances or get disputes resolved, ask your mortgage lender to order a "rapid rescore." This updates your file in 3–5 business days instead of waiting a full billing cycle. Costs $25–$50 per bureau — worth every dollar.

**2. The Authorized User Shortcut**
You don't need to be a primary cardholder to benefit from someone else's good history. Getting added as an authorized user on a card that's been open for 5+ years with low utilization can add decades of positive history to your profile overnight.

**3. Credit Utilization Timing**
Most people don't know: credit card companies report your balance to bureaus on your *statement closing date* — not your payment due date. Pay your balance down **before** the statement closes. Even if you use the card, the bureau only sees what's reported on closing day.

**4. The Goodwill Letter**
For late payments with an otherwise clean history: write a short, honest letter to the creditor asking them to remove the late payment as a "goodwill adjustment." Call it a one-time mistake. About 30–40% of creditors will comply — especially if you've been a customer for years.

**5. Dispute Everything That's Wrong (Even Small Things)**
Bureaus have 30 days to verify disputed items. If they can't verify, they must delete it. Wrong address? Wrong account number? Wrong balance? All disputable. Use all three bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) simultaneously.

**6. Don't Close Old Accounts**
Closing a credit card removes its history from your utilization calculation and can shorten your average account age. Even a card you don't use — keep it open. Use it once every 3 months for a small purchase to prevent the issuer from closing it.

**7. Stack Disputes with Debt Validation**
If a collection account was sold to a third-party collector, you have the right under the FDCPA to demand "debt validation" in writing within 30 days of their first contact. If they can't validate (many third-party collectors buy accounts with incomplete records), they must stop collecting and the item can be removed.

### Traps That Reset the Clock

Avoid these — they erase weeks of progress:

- **Opening new credit cards to "build credit"** — each hard inquiry drops your score 5–10 pts and signals financial stress
- **Settling debt instead of paying in full** — "settled" still shows negative; negotiate "paid in full" or "pay for delete" only
- **Using credit repair companies** — everything they do, you can do free; many charge $99–$299/month for dispute letters you can write in 10 minutes
- **Paying collections without negotiating deletion** — paying a collection doesn't remove it; it just changes status to "paid collection" (still negative for 7 years)
- **Closing cards after paying them off** — keeps utilization high on remaining cards, drops average account age
